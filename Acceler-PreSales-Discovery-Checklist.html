Why They're Here

What's the actual problem they're trying to solve?Not "we need AI training" — what pain triggered this conversation?
What would "success" look like 3 months from now?Get a number: hours saved, adoption %, people certified, something measurable
Who decides — and who pays?Buyer, champion, end-users. Are they the same person or different?
Have they tried AI training before?If yes — what worked, what flopped? Don't repeat someone else's mistake.
Is this a pilot or the full thing?Pilot = smaller scope, prove-it-first energy. Full rollout = bigger deal, longer timeline.

Who's in the Room

What do the participants actually do day-to-day?Job titles are useless. "They close monthly books" or "they manage a sales pipeline" — that's what we need.
How many people, how many batches?20 people in 1 cohort is very different from 500 across 10 cohorts.
Are they technical or non-technical?Determines whether we pitch AI Enabler (non-tech) or AI Builder (hands-on). Biggest fork in the road.
Have they used AI at all? Even casually?"Never touched it" vs "dabbling with ChatGPT" vs "already using Copilot daily" — changes Day 1 completely.
Is it one function or mixed? (e.g., HR + Finance + Sales in same room)Mixed cohorts need broader examples. Single-function cohorts go deeper.

When & How

Virtual or in-person? Where are the people?Time zones matter. "7am PST with people in India" is a very different session than "everyone in one room."
How much time do they have? Half-day, 1 day, 2 days, or more?Don't propose a 3-day programme if they can only spare an afternoon.
When do they want to start? Any hard deadline?Fiscal year end, board commitment, upcoming conference — drives urgency.
Language? English only, or do they need anything else?

Tools & Access

What AI tools do their people have access to today?ChatGPT? Copilot? Claude? Free tier or enterprise? If they have nothing, we need to plan for that.
Is IT going to block anything? Any security restrictions on AI?Some companies block ChatGPT entirely. Some only allow Copilot. Find out BEFORE you design the curriculum.
Do we teach on their tools or ours? Who provides licences?If they have Copilot, we teach on Copilot. If they have nothing, we bring ChatGPT.
Do they need hands-on labs or sandboxes?Builder programmes need labs. Enabler programmes just need a browser + AI tool.
Do they have an LMS? (Cornerstone, Docebo, SAP, etc.)Affects content hosting, completion tracking, and whether we use their platform or ours.
What chat tool for the cohort? (Slack, Teams, WhatsApp)

What They Want Built

What should participants be able to DO after the programme?Not "understand AI" — what specific task? "Draft a variance report with Copilot" or "Build a no-code agent."
Should examples and exercises be from their industry?Generic AI examples are fine for awareness. Industry-specific exercises land 10x harder.
Do they want a final project or capstone?Real business problem (harder to set up, bigger impact) or simulated scenario (faster, still effective)?
How do they want to measure it worked?Pre/post quiz scores? Manager feedback? Adoption metrics at 30/60/90 days? Agree now, not after.
Any data or content they CAN'T use in exercises?Regulated industries have rules. No client PII, no real financials, no patient data in exercises.

Money & Timeline

What's the budget range?Don't propose a Ferrari if they're shopping for a Honda. Saves everyone's time.
When do they need to decide? Who approves?If the decision is next week, don't take 3 weeks to send a proposal.
Are they talking to other vendors?If yes, ask what they're comparing. Helps you position.
Does procurement / legal need to review? How long does that take?Some companies close in a week. Some take 3 months through procurement. Plan accordingly.
